On 10. August, 2024, the Path Undefined blog is online, again... Yes, I had another version of Path Undefined. And now I'm just in the mood to start everything over, it is partially because I wanted to forget something unpleasant, but more importantly, I have noticed that I have slowly figured out what I really wanted to do.

In the past year, I have almost spent all my money, quit a well-paid job and given up my boring but also stable life for nothing but a huge pile of unpaid bills, and I finally realized that things won't work out like this. Now, I'm trying hard to find a job in a big city with a huge stress of competition, which is not easy at all, and the unstable economic situation just makes it worse.

But at least, I've got my peaceful life back again. Sometimes, I just don't realize the importance of something to me, until I lose it.

I hated stability, and I was always looking for new challenges and new excitements. Once something is becoming a routine, I don't like it anymore. Because routine means repetitiveness and restriction, which are boring. That's why I was always trying different new things in different domains, getting to know it, learning it, getting used to it, and dropping it, and then looking for my next challenge.

Modern culture isn't making things easier. People are talking about jumping out of one's comfort zone, chasing whatever one really wants. Without thinking about what exactly the comfort zone is, and where one can find his true passion. This gave me a positive feedback of my thinking pattern, and made me believe that I constantly need new challenges in different domains.

I was fed up with IT, since the job that I had was dull, repetitive and boring. And I have also had an overview about any aspects in IT based on my experiences – they are all just data flows. The data comes from one place, goes to another, and my work is just to receive it, transform, and calculate it, and then send it to or store it in another place. All these factors created a sort of existence crisis inside me. I thought the solution was to start completely new in a different field. However, I couldn't be more wrong. I indeed started a business in a completely new field in a wimp, and even not for myself. Working in the new field didn't solve my existence crisis, but only made it worse. It also made me soak in a toxic environment everyday, made me poor, sick and depressed. In the end, I was only more broken than before.

There are always two dimensions of challenge, the first one is to discover a new world, and the second one is to explore the known world more. I was too focused on discovering new worlds, but completely forgot the other dimension.

By a very occasional chance, I went to a bookstore and saw the IT books on the shelves. I felt ashamed. Years ago, I was proud of myself knowing almost all the topics of the IT books in a bookstore, but now it's not the case anymore, despite that I kept learning in my work. The fact is, more than half of the books are talking about something that I have no ideas about. This gave me a click in my head. At that moment I realized what I actually wanted was a deeper understanding in the IT area.

To explore the known world is even more challenging than to discover a new world. The further I go, the more complex the situation gets. And I will be facing the combination of possibilities, and have to try them out one by one. I have to find different creative ways to solve the problem, and also be patient doing the repetitive tasks.

But there is a prerequisite for this kind of discovery, which is stability, because each step that I will make is strongly based on the previous step that I have made. Once something becomes a routine, it doesn't mean that I have to abandon it and look for something else, but I should turn it into a strong base, so that I can reach somewhere which is higher than that.

And always discovering new worlds can be boring too, because how I get into a new world will become a routine. After having explored several different domains, I will learn a way to get started with something very quickly. But the true challenge is actually how do I overcome the difficulties to stick to the path that I'm taking and go further and further.

Now I don't hate stability anymore, instead, I treasure it. I understand the importance of stability now, not only stability of life, but stability overall. I learned it the hard way.
